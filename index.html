Frequently Asked Questions

CineDraft is a fantasy movie league where you draft films and compete based on real user ratings from Letterboxd, IMDb, and TMDB. Create or join a league, draft movies with friends, make trades, and see who has the best movie taste!
Tap the '+' button on the Home screen, choose 'Create League', enter your league details, and customize your settings. You'll receive an invite code to share with friends.
Tap 'Join League' on the Home screen and enter the invite code provided by your league commissioner.
Yes! You can join as many leagues as you want and switch between them using the League Switcher in the top navigation.
CineDraft runs on a full calendar year (Jan 1 – Dec 31). Each season has 6 bi-monthly draft windows, so your league drafts multiple times throughout the year.
Yes! CineDraft offers Home Screen widgets including Trending Movies, Watchlist, and Unread Messages. Add them from your Home Screen by long-pressing and tapping the '+' button.
Yes! CineDraft is free to download and play.
Only the league commissioner can start a draft. Go to the Draft tab and tap 'Start Draft' when all members are ready.
The draft order is randomly shuffled before each draft and follows a snake format — the pick order reverses every round so the last picker in one round picks first in the next.
If auto-pick is enabled in your league settings, the highest-rated available movie will be automatically selected for you.
Browse upcoming releases, check release dates, and build your draft queue ahead of time. Your queue lets you rank the movies you want so you're ready when your pick comes up.
Look for movies with strong critical potential, popular directors, and favorable release dates. Balance blockbusters with critically acclaimed films for the best results.
Go to League Settings and share your league's invite code. Members can also share the code if 'Members Can Invite' is enabled.
Yes! Go to League Settings, tap 'Transfer' next to the Commissioner section, and select a new commissioner from your league members.
Commissioners can create bonus proposals for the league to vote on, such as bonus points for specific achievements. Members vote on whether to approve these custom scoring bonuses.
Your CineDraft score is based on real user ratings from three platforms: Letterboxd (60%), IMDb (20%), and TMDB (20%). As people on those sites rate a movie, the weighted average updates your score automatically.
CineDraft scores are driven by real user ratings from Letterboxd (film enthusiasts), IMDb (general audiences), and TMDB (community ratings). Rotten Tomatoes and Metacritic scores are also displayed for reference, but they don't affect your CineDraft score.
Major awards like Oscars, Golden Globes, and BAFTAs can provide bonus points. The exact bonuses are configured in your league's scoring system.
Yes! Tap on any movie in your roster to see a detailed score breakdown showing the individual ratings from each source and how they contribute to your total score.
Go to the Trades tab, tap 'Propose Trade', select the movies you're offering and requesting, then send the proposal to another league member.
Yes! When you receive a trade proposal, you can send a counter-offer with different terms instead of simply accepting or rejecting it.
The trade deadline is set by your league commissioner. After the deadline, no new trades can be proposed or accepted.
Tap on any member's profile and select 'Send Message' to start a private conversation. You can also start a new message from the Messages tab.
Tap and hold any message to add an emoji reaction. Quick reactions include thumbs up, heart, laughing, fire, surprised, and thumbs down.

The Journal is your personal movie diary. Log films you've watched with the date, your rating, and notes. It's organized by month so you can look back at your viewing history.
Thoughts are short takes you can share about movies or people. Each Thought has a heat score based on engagement — the more likes and replies it gets, the higher the heat.

Award Show Pools let you compete with friends by predicting winners at major ceremonies like the Oscars. Create or join a pool, make your predictions, and see who's most accurate!
